# PCB Etching Process (Ferric Chloride Method)

This document explains the step-by-step procedure used to fabricate a PCB using ferric chloride.

---

## 🧰 Materials Required

- Copper-clad PCB board
- Ferric chloride solution (FeCl₃)
- Permanent marker / toner transfer print
- Plastic container
- Gloves
- Water
- Sandpaper (optional)
- Drill (optional)

---

## ⚙️ Step-by-Step Procedure

### 1. PCB Preparation
- Clean the copper surface using sandpaper or scrub.
- Ensure the surface is free from dust and grease.

---

### 2. Drawing the Circuit
- Draw the required circuit tracks using a permanent marker  
  **OR**
- Transfer printed layout using toner transfer method.

---

### 3. Preparing Etching Solution
- Pour ferric chloride into a plastic container.
- Do not use metal containers.

---

### 4. Etching Process
- Immerse the PCB in the ferric chloride solution.
- Gently shake or stir the container for uniform etching.
- Wait until unwanted copper is completely removed.

---

### 5. Cleaning the PCB
- Remove the PCB from solution.
- Wash thoroughly with water.

---

### 6. Removing Marker
- Use thinner or acetone to remove marker ink.
- This reveals clean copper tracks.

---

### 7. Drilling (Optional)
- Drill holes for component mounting.

---

## 🔍 Observations

- Etching time depends on solution concentration and temperature
- Uneven etching may occur if not stirred properly
- Over-etching can damage tracks

---

## ⚠️ Precautions

- Handle ferric chloride carefully
- Avoid prolonged exposure to skin
- Do not inhale fumes
- Dispose chemicals responsibly

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

The ferric chloride method is a simple and cost-effective way to fabricate PCBs manually, suitable for small-scale and educational projects.
